About Me

Hi, my name is Chelsea Ogbonna. I was born and raised in Nigeria, where I lived until I was 12 years old before moving to America. Growing up in Nigeria, I witnessed firsthand the challenges of limited access to healthcare and technology, which inspired me to explore how these fields can be combined to create innovative solutions for underserved communities. I saw classmates who couldn’t afford medical care and loved ones suffering from illnesses that could have been easily treated if the medical infrastructure were more stable. These experiences shaped my perspective and motivated me to pursue a career focused on improving healthcare accessibility.

In high school, I discovered a strong passion for biology and mathematics. I was captivated by how biology revealed the intricate workings of the human body, while math provided the tools to analyze and solve complex problems. These subjects opened my eyes to the potential of using science and analytical thinking to develop impactful solutions. My growing interest in merging these fields led me to apply to Northwestern University for Biomedical Engineering.

Throughout my college journey, I have focused on building the skills and knowledge needed to achieve my long-term goal of contributing to healthcare systems in underserved regions. I am particularly driven by the vision of returning to Nigeria to help strengthen and rebuild aspects of its medical community through sustainable engineering solutions.
