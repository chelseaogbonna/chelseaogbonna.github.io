🚀 My Journey

🇳🇬 Growing Up in Nigeria

Living in Nigeria gave me firsthand exposure to the realities of healthcare disparities. Watching friends, classmates, and family members struggle to access quality medical care shaped my desire to create engineering solutions that make healthcare more accessible.

🧬 Discovering Science

In high school I developed a love for biology and mathematics. I became fascinated by how the human body works and how engineering principles could be used to solve medical challenges.

🎓 Northwestern University

At Northwestern University, I pursued a Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering while simultaneously earning a Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering. This combination allowed me to connect medicine, mechanics, manufacturing, and product design into one multidisciplinary engineering approach.

🔬 Research & Innovation

Throughout college, I immersed myself in research spanning implantable pacemakers, microfluidic devices, biomechanics, wearable sensing, robotics, CAD design, manufacturing, and biomedical experimentation. Every project strengthened my belief that engineering has the power to improve patient outcomes.

🚀 Today

Today, I continue developing engineering solutions that bridge research and real-world impact. My goal is to design technologies that improve healthcare accessibility while advancing innovation in medical devices.

💡 What I Love Building

🌍 Beyond Engineering

Outside of engineering, I enjoy mentoring students, leading multidisciplinary teams, and creating opportunities that help others grow. Whether through research, leadership, or outreach, I value collaboration and believe that innovation is strongest when people from different backgrounds work together.

I'm also passionate about giving back to my community and hope to inspire future engineers—especially those who may not have always seen themselves represented in STEM.

🎯 What's Next

My long-term goal is to develop medical technologies that improve patient care around the world, particularly in underserved communities. I hope to work at the intersection of research, engineering, and product development, bringing ideas from concept to real-world clinical impact.

As I continue growing as an engineer, I'm excited to contribute to multidisciplinary teams tackling some of healthcare's most meaningful challenges.
