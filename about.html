Growing up in Nigeria, I witnessed firsthand how limited access to healthcare could dramatically affect people's lives. Seeing preventable illnesses go untreated inspired me to pursue engineering—not simply to build technology, but to create solutions that improve healthcare accessibility and patient outcomes.


I grew up witnessing gaps in healthcare access, which drives my focus on developing engineering solutions for underserved communities.

🚀 My Journey

Nigeria → US Transition — Early exposure to healthcare disparities shaped my motivation. Living in Nigeria gave me firsthand exposure to the realities of healthcare disparities. Watching friends, classmates, and family members struggle to access quality medical care shaped my desire to create engineering solutions that make healthcare more accessible.
Undergraduate Engineering Foundation — Developed foundation in biomedical and mechanical engineering. At Northwestern University, I pursued a Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering while simultaneously earning a Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering. This combination allowed me to connect medicine, mechanics, manufacturing, and product design into one multidisciplinary engineering approach.
Discovering Science & Research Experience — Work in microfluidics, implants, and biomechanics systems. In high school I developed a love for biology and mathematics. Throughout college, I immersed myself in research spanning implantable pacemakers, microfluidic devices, biomechanics, wearable sensing, robotics, CAD design, manufacturing, and biomedical experimentation.
Current Focus & Vision — Translating engineering principles into healthcare solutions. Today, I continue developing engineering solutions that bridge research and real-world impact. My goal is to design technologies that improve healthcare accessibility while advancing innovation in medical devices.
