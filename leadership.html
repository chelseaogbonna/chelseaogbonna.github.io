❤️ My Leadership Philosophy

To me, leadership isn't about having a title—it's about creating an environment where people feel supported, challenged, and empowered to succeed. I strive to lead with empathy, curiosity, and accountability while encouraging collaboration and continuous growth.

Whether I'm organizing campus events, mentoring fellow students, or leading research projects, my goal is always the same: help people reach their potential while working together to solve meaningful problems.

🟨 National Society of Black Engineers

Academic Excellence Chair

As Academic Excellence Chair for the National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) for the 2024-2025 at Northwestern University, I focused on strengthening academic support, encouraging collaboration, and building a stronger engineering community.

I organized programs that connected students across multiple engineering organizations, helping create spaces where students could learn from one another, build relationships, and succeed together.

One of my favorite initiatives was organizing a collaborative study session that brought together students from NSBE, SHPE, SWE, and WIC. More than 50 students attended, creating an inclusive environment where students could study together, share resources, and build relationships across organizations.

Through intentional outreach, event planning, and social media engagement, I also helped increase NSBE meeting attendance by more than 60 students, strengthening chapter involvement and creating a more connected engineering community.

Cross-Organization Collaboration

One of my proudest accomplishments was helping organize a collaborative study session that united students from multiple engineering organizations. Instead of studying separately, students from NSBE, SHPE, SWE, and WIC came together to prepare for exams, exchange knowledge, and support one another. This event demonstrated how collaboration—not competition—creates stronger engineering communities.

💜 Purple Passion

Co-Founder

Seeing a need for greater representation of majorette culture on campus, I helped co-found Purple Passion, Northwestern University's majorette dance team. What started as an idea quickly grew into a thriving student organization.

I recruited members, helped establish the team's foundation, organized practices, coordinated performances, and worked closely with other student leaders to create an inclusive and welcoming environment.

Building an organization from the ground up taught me that leadership isn't just about managing people—it's about creating opportunities, fostering community, and inspiring others to believe in a shared vision.

🔬 Research Leadership

Team Lead — Syncytial Knot Isolation Project

Leadership extends beyond student organizations. As the team lead for a microfluidic device design project, I coordinated project planning, delegated responsibilities, and kept our team aligned throughout the engineering design process.

Our goal was to develop a novel microfluidic platform capable of isolating syncytial knots from placental tissue, a challenge with important applications in maternal and fetal health research. Leading this project required balancing technical problem-solving with communication, project management, and collaboration.

From brainstorming design concepts and creating CAD models to planning experiments and presenting our work, I learned that successful engineering depends just as much on teamwork and communication as it does on technical expertise.
